Greek Ribs

Transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed shores of Greece with our tantalizing Greek Ribs! Bursting with Mediterranean flavors, these succulent ribs are marinated in a symphony of aromatic herbs, zesty lemon, and fragrant garlic, then slow-cooked to tender perfection. Each bite is a culinary journey through the ancient streets of Athens, where the aroma of grilled meats fills the air and the essence of Greek cuisine dances on your palate. Whether enjoyed as a centerpiece for a festive feast or a flavorful weeknight dinner, our Greek Ribs promise to infuse your table with the warmth and vibrancy of the Mediterranean. Opa!

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : meat breakfast pancakes food

instructions

step 1

Marinate the Ribs: Begin by preparing the marinade. In a bowl, combine olive oil, freshly squeezed lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, chopped fresh rosemary, salt, and pepper. Place the ribs in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them, ensuring they are well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse.

step 2

Preheat and Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the ribs from the marinade and let any excess drip off. Place the ribs on the preheated grill and cook for 15-20 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and have developed a golden-brown crust, basting occasionally with any remaining marinade.

step 3

Serve and Enjoy: Once the ribs are cooked to your desired level of doneness, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. Then, transfer them to a cutting board and slice them between the bones into individual portions. Serve the Greek ribs hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley and lemon wedges on the side for an extra burst of citrus flavor. Enjoy this delicious taste of Greece with your favorite sides, such as roasted potatoes, Greek salad, or grilled vegetables. Opa!
